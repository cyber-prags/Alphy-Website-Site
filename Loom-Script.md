# Alphard — Loom Walkthrough Script

**Total runtime target:** 5–7 minutes
**Audience:** VP of Sales / CRO / Head of Account Management at a B2B SaaS company with 50+ AMs and a multi-product catalog
**Goal:** Make them feel the pain of their current "AM-by-spreadsheet" reality, then show how Alphard turns expansion into a repeatable pipeline they can run like new business.

---

## How to record this

- 1440×900 browser window, dock hidden, notifications off
- Sign in once before recording so the personalised state ("Welcome, Joe") is already cached
- Toggle the Light theme on the marketing page; Dark on the workspace (it looks more "product")
- Talk fast but pause for 1 second after each click — the animations are the demo
- Don't read the bullets verbatim; they're the beats, not a teleprompter

---

## SECTION 1 — Hook (0:00 – 0:25)

**Screen:** Your face only, no screenshare yet.

**Script:**

> "Hey — I'm Pragyan, founder of Alphard. Quick context before I show you the product:
>
> Every B2B SaaS company I talk to has the same broken loop. Net new pipeline is run like a machine — Salesforce, sequencers, dashboards, the works. But the moment a deal closes and an Account Manager takes it over, it falls into a spreadsheet. Renewal risk, expansion plays, champion changes — all tracked in someone's head or a Notion page from 2023.
>
> Alphard fixes that. We turn your installed base into a ranked pipeline your AMs work every morning. Let me show you."

**On-camera cue:** Smile, then switch to screenshare.

## SECTION 7 — White Space Matrix (4:30 – 5:00)

**Screen:** Scroll down on the account page to the **White Space** section, or click that tab.

**What to show:**
1. Product × Department grid
2. Active cells (green), trial (blue), expansion targets (accent), gaps (faint)
3. Click a gap cell → CellDetail expands with estimated ARR, confidence, pattern matches
4. Recommendations section below — "Build expansion case" button

**Script:**

> "Multi-product accounts only. This is the white-space matrix — every product in your catalog versus every department at this account. Green cells are sold. Blue is trial. The faint cells are gaps — money you haven't asked for yet.
>
> Click any gap and we tell you the estimated ARR, the confidence based on pattern-matching against similar accounts who did adopt, and a one-click 'build the expansion case' that drafts the business case with comparable wins and ROI math."

## SECTION 10 — How we're different (6:00 – 6:45)

**Screen:** A simple split-screen comparison slide, OR stay on the manager home and overlay your camera bigger. (If you want a slide, build a single Keynote frame with a 5-row comparison table — see PDF for the layout.)

**What to show:**
1. The names of the tools they probably already use or have evaluated
2. One sentence on what each does
3. One sentence on why Alphard is the layer they're missing

**Script:**

> "Quick word on where we sit, because you're probably thinking about a few other tools right now.
>
> **Gainsight, ChurnZero, Catalyst** — those are CS platforms. Built for CSMs and retention. They'll tell you an account is unhealthy. They will not tell your AM where the next $200K of expansion is hiding. Different audience, different job.
>
> **DemandFarm** and the older Key Account Management tools — they're planning surfaces. Org charts and white-space maps that someone updates once a quarter. We respect the planning layer; we add the daily signal layer on top of it, so the plan actually drives action between QBRs.
>
> **Salesforce or HubSpot** — that's the system of record. It stores what happened. It doesn't tell your AM what to do at 8am. We sit on top of it and turn the data into a ranked queue.
>
> **Clari, Gong, Outreach** — forecasting, conversation intelligence, sequencing. Each one is a single signal source. We fuse them — usage, calls, support, calendar, LinkedIn — into one heat score per account, with the reasons explained.
>
> The honest answer to 'what is Alphard?' is: we're the expansion-pipeline layer that doesn't exist in any of those tools. If your AMs are already a spreadsheet plus seven tabs, that's us, replaced with one screen."

**On-camera cue:** Calm, not defensive. You're not attacking these tools — you're acknowledging the prospect already has them and explaining where you slot in.

## Cuts you can make if you need to hit 3 minutes

Drop in this order:
1. Section 7 (White Space) — only relevant for multi-product prospects
2. Section 9 (CRO view) — keep only if pitching a manager
3. Section 4 (Portfolio Quadrant) — show one screenshot instead of demo'ing live

Keep at all costs: Sections 1, 3, 5, 10. Those carry the value prop.

## Variants

**For a CSM-led org:** Swap Section 3's example from "renewal in 71 days" to "usage dropped 22% on the analytics module" and frame it as save-the-account, not expand-the-account. The product is the same; the language changes.

**For a single-product company:** Skip Section 7 entirely. Lean harder on Sections 5 and 6.

**For a CFO in the room:** Add a 30-second tail after Section 9 — "Here's the math. If your AM team books $40M of expansion this year and we lift attainment 8%, that's $3.2M of incremental ARR. Our list price for a team your size is well under that."
