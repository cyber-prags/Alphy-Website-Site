# Alphard — GTM Playbook for the First Customer

**Stage:** Pre-revenue · Design partner hunt
**Time horizon:** 90 days to first paid pilot
**Founder bandwidth:** ~80% on GTM, ~20% on product
**Goal:** 3 paid design partners by day 90 at $2–4K/mo each

---

## Strategic posture

You are pre-revenue with a working prototype. You do not have a brand, customer logos, or case studies. You have three asymmetric advantages: (1) a polished personalised sandbox, (2) a specific founder POV on an expensive, named problem, and (3) the ability to move faster than any incumbent.

The right play is to weaponise those three things and skip every step that requires brand or scale. **Compress the funnel.** Most early-stage SaaS pitches end at "hop on a 30-minute call." Yours ends at "here's a sandbox with your company name on it that you can play with for a week."

This document is a deliberate set of bets, not a list of options. The "hold off" section is as important as the "do" section — most early founders die from doing too many things, not from doing the wrong thing.

---

## ICP — narrow on purpose

| Filter | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Industry | Multi-product B2B SaaS | PLG / consumer / single-product startups |
| Revenue | $20M–$200M ARR | < $20M (no AM team) or > $300M (procurement nightmare) |
| Team | 50–200 AMs (or CSMs doing expansion) | < 20 (founder still owns accounts) |
| Geography | US or UK HQ | EU/APAC for now (timezone friction) |
| Catalog | 3+ products / SKUs | Single-product (white space loses meaning) |
| Pain marker | NRR < 110% in last public quarter | NRR > 130% (they don't feel pain) |

**Hot prospect signals** — accounts with at least one of these jump to the top of the list:
- Just had a RIF that consolidated AM/CSM into one team
- Just hired a Head of Customer / VP Account Management (90-day mandate window)
- Publicly missed NRR target last quarter
- Recently raised a Series C/D — pressure to grow expansion ARR
- Posted a job for "Director of Revenue Operations — Customer" or similar

**Anti-ICP:** Do not chase F500. Do not chase pre-Series-B. Do not chase companies whose AM team is < 10 people. You will burn three months and learn nothing.

## The wedge

**The sandbox is the entire GTM motion.** Internalise this.

Why it works:
- **Removes calendar friction.** Champions can play with it on their own time, without pulling their boss in.
- **Beats demo theatre.** They control the clicks. They form their own opinion. They become your internal seller.
- **Creates an "oh, this actually works" moment** that no live demo replicates.
- **Lets you stay on offence.** While they're in the sandbox, you're already on the next ten prospects.

Every piece of outbound ends with the same call to action: "Reply with your name and company and I'll spin you a sandbox." Not a Calendly link. Not a deck. Not a discovery call.

Build this once, properly:
- Personalisation flow already shipped (Welcome, {firstName})
- Sandbox seeded with 3 accounts that mirror their real customer base (research before sending)
- 90-second personalised Loom included with every send (re-record, don't reuse — it's the proof you care)
- Day-3 nurture (PostHog event-triggered) if they haven't logged in
- Day-7 wrap-up email with a "what would it take to get this in front of your team?" CTA

# Sales motion

**Founder-led only.** No SDRs, no AEs, no closers. Until you have personally closed five deals, you cannot teach the motion to anyone.

## The 7-step sequence

1. **Identify** (10 net new ICP prospects per week, hand-picked from LinkedIn)
2. **Warm up** (comment thoughtfully on 2–3 of their posts before reaching out — never a generic "great post!")
3. **Reach out** (LinkedIn DM > cold email; 80–120 words; one specific observation about their company; sandbox offer at the end)
4. **Personalised sandbox** (spin it up, send the 90-second Loom)
5. **Trial week** (intervene at day 3 if they haven't logged in; share a usage screenshot at day 5)
6. **"What would it take" call** (30 min — not a demo. They've seen the demo. This is "tell me what would have to be true for you to write a $24K cheque?")
7. **Pilot proposal** (6-month paid pilot, $2–4K/mo, success criteria pre-agreed in writing)

## Outreach template (LinkedIn DM)

> Hey [Name] — saw your post on [specific thing]. The bit about [specific detail] mirrors what I keep hearing from VPs running expansion at companies your size: the AMs are working off a spreadsheet and a hunch.
>
> I'm building Alphard — it ranks every account in your book daily by expansion heat and tells your AMs which one to work first, with reasons. Not another CS platform.
>
> Easier to show than describe. If I sent you a 7-day sandbox with your company name on it and three accounts that look like your real ones — would you click around for ten minutes and tell me if it's any good? No call required.
>
> — Pragyan

What this template does right:
- Names a specific observation (not "I noticed your company is great")
- Anchors the pain in their language
- States what Alphard is **not** (CS platform) before what it is
- Asks for ten minutes of clicks, not a meeting
- Founder voice, not corporate

## Pricing for the first five customers

- **List price**: don't publish one yet
- **Pilot price**: $2,000–$4,000/month, 6-month minimum, billed monthly
- **Annual ACV target**: $24K–$48K
- **Discounting authority**: you are the founder; discount whatever you need to. The cheque is the validation, not the revenue.

You will lose money on these deals after support and integration time. That is fine. You are buying signal, case studies, and product feedback at below market rate.

# Marketing motion

**Pull, not push.** Founder personality first; brand later. Every dollar that goes to "marketing" before you have 10 paid customers is a dollar that should have gone to outbound time.

## Channels (in priority order)

### 1. LinkedIn personal brand — primary
- 3 posts per week, all on expansion / AM ops / multi-product growth
- Show your thinking, not your product. Product mentions are <1 in 5 posts.
- Comment thoughtfully on every post by your top 50 ICP prospects within 48 hours
- Reply to every DM personally within 4 working hours
- **Goal:** become the LinkedIn voice on AM expansion within 6 months

### 2. Long-form essay every 2 weeks — secondary
- 1,000–1,500 words on a specific expansion-ops problem
- Cross-post: LinkedIn article → blog → email to friendly readers (start a curated list of 50–100 names you've met)
- Light SEO: target 5–10 specific phrases a year, not a content factory

### 3. The product is the marketing — always-on
- Every demo URL, every Loom, every animated screenshot in the app is shareable.
- Ask every sandbox user "would you mind if I shared an anonymised screenshot of your trial?" — most say yes.
- Build a "show, don't tell" library you draw from in posts.

### 4. Podcast guest spots — one per month
- Get on AM-adjacent and CS-adjacent shows. Not the giants — the specific niche ones (50–200 listeners is fine if they are all your ICP).
- Pitch with the angle, not the product: "I have a contrarian take on why CS platforms fail AMs" beats "I built an AM tool, want to talk about it?"

### 5. Hand-picked newsletter sponsorships — selective
- $500–$2,000 per placement
- Only if the newsletter has < 50K subs and audience is clean ICP
- Test 3 placements before scaling spend on any one
- Avoid "RevOps" / "SaaStr" general lists — too broad

## Content themes that compound

| Theme | Example angle | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Positioning | "Why your CSM tool isn't helping your AMs" | Names the incumbent, gives them a frame |
| POV | "The expansion playbook nobody writes down" | Founder-as-thinker, not founder-as-vendor |
| Build-in-public | "We rebuilt the AM dashboard from scratch — here's what we learned" | Product without selling |
| Data | "I read every public NRR disclosure this quarter — here's the pattern" | Earned authority, sharable, evergreen |
| Manifestos | "Expansion is a pipeline, not a relationship" | Anchors your category |

# GTM motion (the funnel mechanics)

## The funnel

| Stage | Definition | Target rate |
|---|---|---|
| Cold contact | DM or email sent to an ICP prospect | — |
| Engaged | Replied, opened sandbox link, or watched the Loom | 8–12% |
| Sandbox active | Logged into the sandbox 2+ times in trial week | 50% of engaged |
| Pilot conversation | 30-minute call booked | 60% of active |
| Pilot signed | $2K+/mo paid pilot live | 30–50% of conversations |

**Math:** 200 cold prospects → ~20 engaged → ~10 active sandboxes → ~6 calls → 2–3 paid pilots over 60–90 days.

This is the realistic shape of founder-led pre-revenue sales. If your numbers are dramatically worse, the ICP is wrong, the wedge is broken, or the outreach copy is off — diagnose in that order.

## Where pipeline comes from (target mix)

- **60% outbound LinkedIn DMs** — the work that gets done in the dark
- **25% inbound from LinkedIn content** — compounds over months
- **15% warm intros** — actively ask every angel, advisor, and ex-colleague for 3 specific intros each

## Tooling (cheap and sufficient)

- **CRM:** Notion or HubSpot free tier. Do not buy Salesforce.
- **Outreach:** ProspectingFlow / Lemlist / Smartlead — pick one, $50–100/mo.
- **Lead source:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/mo) — non-negotiable.
- **Analytics:** PostHog (already shipped) for sandbox usage tracking.
- **Email:** Gmail with a tracking pixel (Streak / Mixmax). Do not buy a sales engagement platform yet.

Total monthly stack cost: < $300. If yours is more, you are over-engineered.

# 90-day execution plan

## Days 1–30 — Foundation

**Goal:** 3 sandbox trials, 1 pilot conversation.

- [ ] ICP list of 200 contacts built (Sales Navigator → spreadsheet)
- [ ] Sandbox personalisation flow audited end-to-end
- [ ] Day-3 and day-7 nurture emails written and triggered
- [ ] 3 LinkedIn essays published; cadence locked at 3 posts/week
- [ ] 50 cold DMs sent (week 1: 10, week 2: 15, week 3: 25)
- [ ] One advisor / angel asked for 3 specific intros

If you have zero replies after 50 DMs, your message is wrong. Rewrite before sending more.

## Days 31–60 — Volume

**Goal:** 10 sandbox trials, 5 pilot conversations, 1 pilot signed.

- [ ] 100 more cold DMs (50 per week)
- [ ] Weekly LinkedIn cadence shipping reliably
- [ ] First podcast booked (even a tiny one)
- [ ] First pilot proposal written and sent
- [ ] Day-30 retro: which channel converted? Double down on it.
- [ ] Customer development call with every sandbox user, even the ones who didn't engage

## Days 61–90 — Conversion

**Goal:** 3 paid pilots live.

- [ ] Optimise the highest-converting channel from days 1–60; cut the others
- [ ] First pilot live; weekly check-in cadence documented
- [ ] Case study draft started (you can write the structure before results land)
- [ ] First public talking point about a customer (anonymised if needed)
- [ ] Decision point: if 0 paid pilots by day 60, the ICP is wrong — re-diagnose, do not just push harder

# Hold off on (and why)

The hardest part of early-stage GTM is saying no to obvious-looking moves. These are all things a "real" company does. You are not a real company yet. Be honest about it.

| Hold | Why | Trigger to revisit |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring AEs / SDRs | You can't teach a motion you haven't validated | After you've personally closed 5 deals |
| Paid Google / LinkedIn ads | Burns cash with no signal pre-PMF | After you have a known CAC and LTV |
| SOC 2 | $20–40K and 6 months; deals don't block on it yet | First deal that explicitly blocks on it |
| Self-serve / Stripe checkout | You need conversations to learn the buyer | After 10 customers with the same buying motion |
| Conference booths | Booth + travel + opportunity cost > lead value | When you can sponsor with a customer logo on the slide |
| Brand redesign | Current brand is fine; iterate after revenue | After 10 customers tell you it looks dated |
| PR firm | No story to tell yet; PR works post-milestone | After Series A or first lighthouse customer |
| Pre-built integrations beyond Salesforce + Gong | Builds debt before product-market fit | When a deal explicitly requires it |
| Partner programme | Pre-revenue; no partner cares | After $1M ARR |
| G2 / Capterra listings | Empty profiles hurt | When you have 5 real reviews to seed |
| ABM platforms (6sense, Demandbase) | Useless under $5M ARR | After scaling outbound past founder bandwidth |
| Head of Marketing hire | You are the marketing | After Series A |
| Multi-channel attribution | Premature; you have 200 prospects | After 1,000 leads/month |
| Webinars (your own) | No audience yet | Co-host after you have 50 customers |

# Metrics that matter

**Track weekly, in this order:**

1. New sandbox trials this week
2. Pilot conversations booked this week
3. Paid pilots live (cumulative)
4. Qualitative replies — every "no" with a reason is data; every "yes" is a story
5. Cycle time: first contact → pilot signed

**Metrics to ignore until further notice:**

- Website traffic
- Email open rates (broken since iOS 15 anyway)
- LinkedIn impressions (vanity unless they convert)
- MQL / SQL labels (you have neither yet)
- CAC (you have no data)
- Burn multiple (you have no revenue)
- ARR projections beyond 90 days

# The "what works best" summary

1. **Personalised sandbox beats scheduled demo call.** Always.
2. **LinkedIn DM beats cold email** for senior buyers in B2B SaaS.
3. **Founder voice beats brand voice** until you have brand to speak with.
4. **Hand-picked 200 beats sprayed 5,000.** Quality of list > volume.
5. **One signed pilot beats ten "great meetings."** A meeting is not progress.

# The "hold off" summary

1. Don't hire sales until you've sold yourself.
2. Don't spend on paid until you know LTV.
3. Don't build certifications until a deal blocks.
4. Don't add channels you can't measure.
5. Don't ship integrations on spec — ship them on demand.

---

# Final word

The biggest risk at this stage is not picking the wrong channel. It's spreading thin across five channels and getting signal from none. Pick LinkedIn-DM-into-sandbox as the only motion for the next 90 days. If it works, scale it. If it doesn't, you'll know in 60 days, not 6 months — and you can change one variable at a time.

Get the first cheque. Everything else is a downstream problem.
